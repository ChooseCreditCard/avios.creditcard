Avios: Shared Airline Currency & Credit Cards

Avios is a shared loyalty currency used by several airline programs. This guide explains how Avios are earned, how redemptions work across different brands, how elite status ties in, and how credit cards can be one of the fastest ways to build a meaningful Avios balance.

Best Cards for Earning Avios

1. What the Avios Ecosystem Is

Unlike a single-airline scheme, Avios is a shared points currency used by multiple airline loyalty programs. Depending on your market, you may encounter Avios in programs such as:

  • British Airways Executive Club
  • Iberia Plus
  • Aer Lingus AerClub
  • Vueling Club and other regional partners

In many cases, you can move Avios between these programs and redeem on Oneworld and other partners, giving the currency a lot of flexibility for European and transatlantic travelers.

2. How Avios Are Earned

You earn Avios through:

  • Flights on the airline linked to your specific Avios-issuing program (for example BA, Iberia or Aer Lingus).
  • Oneworld partner flights credited to your chosen Avios account.
  • Co-branded credit cards that earn Avios on every purchase.
  • Hotels, car rentals and shopping portals that award Avios.
  • Occasional promotions that provide bonus Avios on specific routes or partners.

For many Avios users, most of the balance comes from credit card spend and shopping partners rather than flights alone.

3. How Credit Cards Boost Avios Earning

Avios-linked credit cards let you turn everyday spending into Avios. Depending on the card and market, you may see:

  • Flat earn rates on general purchases.
  • Bonus categories like travel, groceries or online shopping.
  • Welcome bonuses after reaching a minimum spend threshold.
  • Companion vouchers or upgrade certificates when you spend above certain annual levels.

Used strategically, these cards can generate enough Avios for long-haul flights even if you do not fly frequently.

4. Redemption Categories Using Avios

Avios can typically be redeemed for:

  • Short-haul flights within Europe or regional networks, often a sweet spot for value.
  • Long-haul flights in economy, premium or business class.
  • Upgrades from one cabin to the next on eligible fares.
  • Part-pay with Avios – reducing the cash price of a ticket.
  • Other travel rewards such as hotels or cars in some markets.

The exact options and value depend on which Avios program you use (BA Exec Club vs Iberia Plus, etc.), but the underlying currency is the same.

5. Elite Status Tiers in Avios Programs

Avios itself is just a currency, while status tiers live inside the individual programs that use Avios. As an example, British Airways Executive Club has:

  • Blue – entry level.
  • Bronze – basic priority benefits.
  • Silver – lounge access and enhanced perks.
  • Gold – top published tier with maximum benefits.

Other Avios-issuing programs have their own tier names and rules, but the structure is similar: the more you fly (and sometimes the more you spend on co-branded cards), the higher your recognition and travel benefits.
